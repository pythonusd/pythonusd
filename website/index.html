Python USD

Lamden's first open source, smart contract controlled stablecoin

Python USD (PUSD) is an algorithmic stablecoin pegged to the US dollar and collateralized by TAU.
All operations involving PUSD, such as issuance and payouts, are fully transparent and governed by a smart contract.

Why use PUSD

Stablecoins aspire to achieve the functions of traditional money without relying on confidence in an issuer—such as a central bank—to stand behind the “money.” – Lael Brainard

We provide traders with a safe harbor, much like gold, which allows them to reduce their risk to crypto-assets without the need to leave the Lamden ecosystem. But we would not need multiple stablecoins, if that was the only reason. Our unique approach allows for 0 slippage, you can swap 1 million USD without a problem.

Difference LUSD / PUSD

LUSD is minted using USDT which is bridged from another chain and is therefore collateralized by USDT on other chains and depends on other users to bridge USDT. PUSD on the other hand lives primarily on Lamden and is collateralized by TAU on the Mainchain. With a couple of genius ideas we manage to keep it fully collateralized by dynamic fees and exchange rates. You can exchange TAU for PUSD and back at any point and as much as you can.
